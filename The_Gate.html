The Gates Of Musphel



Proper Etiquette For Approaching The Gates

This chapter of the tome goes over how you should dress, act, speak, offer, and behave when you travel to The Gates Of Musphel.


Proper Dress:

When approaching the gates, proper attire must be worn at all times.

One should always care to look their best when you embark on a journey to the gates.

Formal attire in the native style from your region is easiest way to achieve this.

Royal garb is highly cautioned, dressing in an overly expensive manner may come off as rude, or haughty.

Because of this, those of higher social status should dress modestly, in attire you would go to a friends manor for tea, for example.

Show Respect to the Gates:

One must acknowledge that they hold no power they once thought they had, in the presence of the gates.

You must always act in deference to the majesty of The Gates Of Musphel.

Much like a King going to visit another nation's ruler, being aware of your standing is pertinent.

Unlike a king however, no matter what standing you are of in the outside realm, in the presence of the gates, you are below everything.

Should you act in a way unbefitting, you would be considered lucky if all that happened was being expelled from the gates.

Offerings:

Bringing a proper offering for the gates is of the utmost importance.

As the gates do not interact with outsiders, save for those who make the journey to the gates, objects of material wealth are worthless.

If you wish to leave the best impression possible, an object of history, or personal importance is required.

Bringing an artifact from your nation's history is good, however you must be sure to study its origin and story surronding it.

If you are unable to tell the tales and importance of your offering, it would be as if you were slinging mud upon the gates.
